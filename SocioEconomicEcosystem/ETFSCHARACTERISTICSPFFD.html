    Introduction & Motivation

    Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) are more popular for providing investors with diversification, daily portfolio transparency and liquidity in a low cost. The feasibility of these investment vehicles is based on the creation and redemption mechanism which is mainly implementing an actionable arbitrage process, see this global review.

    For international diversification, ETFs tracking foreign equity indexes, are the most suitable for investors to gain exposure to international securities. Their supposed genuine arbitrage process of creating and redeeming shares with the provided transparency of ETFs underlying securities/indexes, may allow investors to adjust a ETF’s market price with its underlying index value even over different times of trading.

    Various factors may hinder the efficiency of this creation and redemption mechanism and its actionable arbitrage process. Factors affecting these processes, such as expectations about monetary policy, inflation, macroeconomic, sentiment and politico-economic factors.

    The exposure to these risk factors may be analyzed in a risk–return portfolio context:
    • Using Stochastic Calculus for performance evaluation to detect Out/Under performing.
    • For Fund Flow Dynamics assessment, the Stochastic Discount Factor Concept (Pricing Kernels) is used and evaluated under a Machine Learning Perspective.
    Given a specific number of ETFs/Indexes (finite-state), their discrete-time price dynamics can be described by an underlying discrete-time finite "Markov chain" specified by a Markov matrix G.

    This Markov matrix G is viewed as a graph with weights in the interval [0,1] and the vertices are representing the considered ETFs/Indexes. A segmentation of these vertices into communicating classes (communities) is conducting to highlight the characteristic proprieties of these ETFs.

    If the Markov matrix G is ergodic, a unique limit stationary distribution which corresponds to their long-run outcome, can be obtained numerically. The estimated values of the limit stationary distribution define the stochastically stable states as the ones having positive probability in the limit stationary distribution.

    Furthermore, the estimated values of the limit stationary distribution provide insights about the dynamics of Markov chains, as sets of states having higher limit stationary distribution values that are linked to each other and may be grouped into different communicating classes.

    These recurrent communicating classes provide insights about crucial steps in the transitions and the ETFs/Indexes prices contain socially and economically factors to explain the behavioral patterns in the transitions. ETFs/Indexes prices are the results of asymmetric information shared between a population of investors having certain preferences and expectations about monetary policy, inflation, macroeconomic, sentiment and politico-economic factors and the recurrent communicating classes reflect their risk averse decisions as aggregate behaviors, one communicating class has liquid ETFs/Indexes and the second has illiquid ETFs/Indexes, for instance.
