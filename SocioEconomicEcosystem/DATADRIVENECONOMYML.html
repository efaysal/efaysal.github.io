Enterprising the Data Economic Indicators

Their Inherent Predictability as an EconoSystem

Using Mathematical Modeling & Machine Learning Tools

Faysal.El.Khettabi@gmail.com

    Economic Indicators considered in this work are related to economics, finance and accounting. Today's socio-economical system mainly relies on these fields which are defined accordingly to capital formation/maintenance and income/earning.

    Key Points:

    Whether a business is a small or large entity, what is doing can be molded by an econosystem which is directly influenced by the time evolving microeconomic and macroeconomic indicators, not necessary in a explicit and linear fashion. Staggering amounts of these data economic indicators are accessible as never before from public sources of open data. Thanks to low cost computing power, the processing capabilities of these economic indicator are accessible to users around the world. It's time to enable business entities with comprehensible and efficient algorithms & analytical tools scattering useful intelligence from these data economic indicators.

    This called process "Enterprising the Data Economic Indicators" is fundamentally different than what has come before, this process is based on the industrialization of learning through the ever-wider deployment of Machine Learning Tools and as companion to the formal economic models used to inform economic policy in concert with experimentation regarding the design of regulatory frameworks.

    Completing and Challenging DSGE Models:

    The need to augment the current dynamic stochastic general equilibrium (DSGE) models with a Machine Learning framework that can help us understand the associations(linear & nonlinear) between these indicators that shape the econosystem in an economy appears a reasonable addition to take the lessons of the recent crisis 2007-2008 into account. Following the unexpected sharp decline in real economic activity in the US and around the world in the autumn of 2008 and spring of 2009, a debated question "are DSGE models still useful in policy analysis?" has been addressed in this article: On the future of macroeconomic models.. The article promotes a clear message: "the current DSGE models are flawed, but they contain the right foundations and must be improved rather than discarded". Holistic approach is encouraged, different types of macro models are needed and have to interact.

    Entry of Economic Machine Learning Models:

    Machine Learning models are also flexible enough to be used for many purposes, very effective in selecting important indicators from large number of candidate indicators(impossible to do with DSGE framework) and are designed to capture information that is true across the majority of repeated samples. Importantly enough, these flexible machine learning techniques have the potential to capture complex nonlinear interaction between the indicators using only algorithmic concepts and considering the interaction mechanisms as unknown and to be discovered by processing the datasets associated to these indicators.

    The nonlinear aspect of these Machine Learning models is very crucial as the monetary policy, mainly conducted by central banks, has an important role in shaping microeconomic & macroeconomic developments into an economical systems affecting both national and around the world levels in an implicit manner with nonlinear feedbacks. For instance, unconventional monetary policy including credit easing, quantitative easing, forward guidance, and signaling.

    Only changes in interest rates with a suitable supply-side credit conditions(credit standards) may have a direct effect on the valuation of financial assets and their expected returns, as well as on the consumption and investment decisions of households and corporations. Therefore sound established decisions by credit standards can in turn have desired consequences as a stable financial system enjoying gross domestic product (GDP) growth, more jobs, and controlled inflation.

    It is important to evaluate quantitatively how these decisions end up affecting the various economic indicators and their inherent predictability as an EconoSystem to address the economyâ€™s performance. A key goal of adding machine learning framework to DSGE framework is to provide insights about the transmission mechanism of monetary policy and to assess the predictive power of a number of economic indicators representing the state of an economy during a specific period of time including Economic Indicators related to economics, finance and accounting as today's socio-economical system mainly relies on these fields which are defined accordingly to capital formation/maintenance and income/earning. These most predictive indicators have then to be modeled accordingly to the fabrics of the DSGE models, dynamic, stochastic, and then to characterize the general equilibrium of the economy. This machine learning interactive process with DSGE models makes them less imperialistic and accept sharing a holistic scene with other general purpose approaches to economical modelization including economics, finance and accounting.

    How to Proceed:

    Our quantitative analysis uses a set of mathematical models to evaluate the historical data related to most popular economic indicators in public databases for patterns that help to answer the questions in the "Main Points Section" in terms of numerical values. These numerical values are presented into a form guided by this statement "Translating and analyzing big data using charts, graphics, and images is becoming more and more necessary for decision-making." promoted by the Association of Public Data Users.

    We provide:
    • Interactive Charting features the temporal dynamics of the considered interest rates in this work and to facilitate their comparisons. To find out more about the temporal dynamic of a specific interest rate, a double click on its name in the legend creates its associated curve and then add up for multiple comparisons.

    • Economic Indicators Network is the results of our mathematical modeling and subsequent computation to study the numerical behavior of these interest rates. The obtained numerical values are used to explore and gain new insights into the impact of the temporal decision that determined them and to estimate and highlight specific interconnection levels between them. If there exist groups of interest rates highly interconnected but with few links to other interest rates. This kind of structure carry out much information about the network to answer our question quantitatively.

    • Random Walks on Interest Rates Network, by considering the interest rates network as a Graph (discrete mathematics), the theory of random walk on this graph help to extract basic properties determined by the spectrum of the graph as its community structures and stationary distribution.

    Interpretation of stationary distribution values

    More the stationary distribution value of a given indicator is higher more this indicator is affecting significantly the values of the considered economic indicators. The indicator in the lowest stationary distribution class have more difficulty to influence significantly other indicator values than the indicator in the highest stationary distribution class.

      IFRS-Elements of Financial Statements, Items & Measurements

      Here, We assess this question: What do we know about the most "relevant and informative" items in IFRS-Elements of Financial Statements?

      Accounting is about Concepts & Mathematics, Machine Learning can fit into what can be considered understanding the conceptual framework of IFRS Financial Statements.
      Mainly using International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS), CANSIM Table 187-0001 "Quarterly balance sheet and income statement, by NAICS" provides readily available numerical data to explore the question using Machine Learning Tools. As time series, the period used is from 2014/12 to 2017/12.
