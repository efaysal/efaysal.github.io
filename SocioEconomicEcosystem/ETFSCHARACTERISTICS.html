    Introduction & Motivation

    Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) are more popular for providing investors with diversification, daily portfolio transparency and liquidity in a low cost. The feasibility of these investment vehicles is based on the creation and redemption mechanism which is mainly implementing an actionable arbitrage process, see this global review.

    For international diversification, ETFs tracking foreign equity indexes, are the most suitable for investors to gain exposure to international securities. Their supposed genuine arbitrage process of creating and redeeming shares with the provided transparency of ETFs underlying securities/indexes, may allow investors to adjust a ETF’s market price with its underlying index value even over different times of trading.

    Various factors may hinder the efficiency of this creation and redemption mechanism and its actionable arbitrage process. Factors affecting these processes, such as expectations about monetary policy, inflation, macroeconomic, sentiment and politico-economic factors.The exposure to these risk factors may be analyzed in a risk–return portfolio context, using Stochastic Discount Factor Models (Pricing Kernels) evaluated under a Machine Learning Perspective.

    In this work, an empirical analysis is conducted using the following Datasets:

    A) Country ETFs vs. Major World Indexes

    iShares funds issued by BlackRock , called country ETFs, i.e. tracking foreign MSCI indexes. The main objective is to automatically segment these country ETFs with respect to major world cap-weighted indexes to gain insights about their international diversification characteristics.
    Importantly enough, these country ETFs are all traded in US markets, among probable risk factors affecting them are fluctuations in foreign/US indexes and the currency gross rate of changes. Moreover, US investors are relying more on the sentiment and information in the home markets, which could lead to more changes into the country ETF's fund flow dynamics.
