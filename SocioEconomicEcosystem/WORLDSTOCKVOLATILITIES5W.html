    Terms: A security is a tradable asset in a financial market, this term refers to any form of financial instrument. In financial stock market, security is termed as stock, asset, equity or share. In this essay, stock is used to refer to security. The degree by which its price fluctuates can be termed as volatility.

    Motivation:

    This work explores suitable analytical forms to calculate volatility of a given stock based on oscillatory processes, i.e, the derivation of these processes uses the stochastic assumptions in the conventional stochastic financial framework to reformulate the problem as a quasi-deterministic framework highlighting and providing insights about the volatility characteristics in terms of stock settings. These oscillatory processes take into consideration the fact that the real world may follow a Jump-Diffusion process. When the new information arrives, the stock value may jump discontinuously(Jump Process) or will not differ significantly from the last value and the new information inflow has only a differential impact(Diffusion Process).

    Empirical Analysis:

    We are not providing here an extensive coverage of all the technicalities of this framework. Our aim is to provide a numerical illustration of our framework to survey depth volatility patterns in a given number of stocks traded in a specific financial stock market(stock exchange). A stock exchange can be considered as a socio-economic mirror where the important changes in the socio-economical features are reflected in the stocks price volatilities, i.e, any increase or decrease in the prices of stocks indicates the boom or bust time period of the economy respectively which may provide a barometer of economic prospects, social movements, and social mood.

    A more effective application of this quasi-deterministic framework, based on these oscillatory processes, is to assess both qualitatively and quantitatively the price volatilities of a number of specific stocks in a given financial stock exchange subject to a well-established regulatory environment***, corporate governance requirements and so on.

    Understanding the price-volatility dynamic via the historical relationship between directional changes of the underlying price and directional changes in volatility in stock markets is an essential retrospective task to assess how this recently installed policy called Volatility Control Mechanism (VCM) is performing. VCM is a measure designed to protect market integrity by preventing extreme price volatility arising from major trading errors and other unusual incidents.

    (*) For instance, the Investment Industry Regulatory Organization of Canada (IIROC) published guidance on the establishment and operation of price thresholds by Canadian marketplaces to further reduce short-term, unexplained price volatility and address risks arising from electronic trading. The guidance took effect on August 25, 2016.

    Volatility Community Structure(VCS):

    The derived numerical features for each stock help to build a volatility network between all the considered stocks traded in the considered stock exchange. Unsupervised Machine Learning methods are used for the detection and identification of communities in this complex volatility network. This community structure groups sets of stocks such that each set of stocks is volatility-connected internally. Each community structure is assigned the averaged features that are computed from the features of stocks belonging to this community structure.
