    Introduction

    The ERC-20 derived token contract aims to be used for crowd-sales. All available tokens are obtained using suitable transactions and associated fees, if only few participants are needed, hence extremely high fees for the transactions are needed to be mined in a timely manner to obtain the proposed tokens. Recent Status ICO crowd-sales caused massive network congestion due to the large volume of transactions which are created in a short space of time. Status ICO also caused headaches for the rest of the Ethereum network.

    Status ICO clogged up the network with a huge number of high gas fee transactions, having more chance to be in a block and preventing low gas fee transactions from getting into the Ethereum blockchain. These low gas fee transactions are just keeping flowing in the network without establishing their intended business purpose as they are not yet in the Ethereum blockchain.

    From the Yellow Paper : " Transactors are free to specify any gasPrice that they wish, however miners are free to ignore transactions as they choose. A higher gas price on a transaction will therefore cost the sender more in terms of Ether and deliver a greater value to the miner and thus will more likely be selected for inclusion by more miners."

    The short probabilistic version of this YP statement can be understood as representing a distribution of probabilities for the transactions to be included soon or later into the blockchain. These distribution of probabilities are defined by the miners accordingly the proposed/observed gasPrices by the transactors.

    These distribution of probabilities are hard to conceive/predict exactly. A batting average is to illustrate these distribution of probabilities assuming for now two kind of fees low(100 transactions) and high(100 transaction), the probability "to be in the blockchain" having only two outcomes, in a block or not.
    • With high fee, 80 transactions end-up into the blockchain, 80%. Given a high fee transaction, the chance to be in a block is 80%.
    • With low fee, 10 transactions end-up into the blockchain, 10%. Given a low fee transaction, the chance to be in a block is 10%.
    This example sounds like a Bernoulli distribution for each type of transactions but in fact our distribution of probabilities are mainly represented as beta distributions(conjugate prior to the Bernoulli distribution). The probability of a transaction to be in a block will be considered as beta-distributed probabilities. Somme illustrative beta distributions are in this Figure.

    The beta distributions are defined on the interval [0, 1] parametrized by two positive shape parameters, denoted by α and β, that appear as exponents of the random variable and control the shape of the distribution.
