    Cohort Formation in Personalized/Precision Medicine

    Important efforts are developing ways to design and build landmark longitudinal data-cohorts for personalized/precision medicine research. These longitudinal data-cohorts will be formed by engaging and organizing a number of participants from a single survival population into data sub-cohorts with a scientific goal to shape efficient predictive models in order to develop a well-defined references "healthy" and "not-healthy" or to find two sub-groups, one with better and the other with worse survival prognosis, for instance.

    The characterization of these sub-groups will be facilitated by using a number of tests based on features associated to clinical, molecular, imaging, sensor, self-reported, behavioral, psychological, environmental and genetic/genemics and so on. A score from a regression of these potential factors(biomarkers) or some other model to characterize the sub-groups can also be used as one unified biomarker.

    When well-integrated, the preprocessed data related to these tests is expected to provide an estimation of the difference in the survival of two prognostic subgroups in the single survival population at any given time.

    Recently, we developed an Informatics Solution to assess numerically the impact of a number of issues on the accuracy of these cohort studies, some illustrations are in these Essays:
    • Difference in the survival of two prognostic subgroups at a given time versus sensitivity/specificity/prevalence.
    • Difference in the survival of two prognostic subgroups with numerical simulation in the presence of censoring.
    • Competing Risks & Time-to-Event Analysis with Censoring.

    We are interested to assess the analytics and the numerics of the cohort formation and its relation of the accuracy and reliability of the project using cohort studies, i.e. impact of censoring on the used formulations to estimate the sensitivity and specificity values and by consequent on the difference in the survival of the prognostic groups from a single survival population.

    We use R Software to generate/analyze this database. R is a software environment for statistical computing and graphics supported by the R Foundation for Statistical Computing . R performs many statistical analyses needed in practical applications: linear and generalized linear models, nonlinear regression models, time-series analysis, parametric and nonparametric tests, clustering, smoothing and survival analysis. R also provides additional packages for specific purposes.
